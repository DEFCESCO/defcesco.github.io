About Austin DeFrancesco

I have more than a decade of experience in cybersecurity and business IT, working across finance, manufacturing, and enterprise environments in roles ranging from system administrator to security architect, penetration tester, red team operator, and detection engineer.

I currently work as a Threat Detection Engineer. Additional information is available upon request.

Origins

My passion for cybersecurity began in 2003 when my father brought home a Dell Alienware Area-51 desktop computer. That machine changed everything. I spent countless middle and high school evenings writing papers on security and cyber policy as homework assignments and presenting them as guest lectures — a habit that set the tone for a career built on curiosity and rigor.

By 2013 I was already in the field, spending summers inside local IT departments, building operational intuition from the ground up. During that same period I launched a server rental venture spanning the US, UK, and Australia, running on a custom LAMP stack and content delivery network.

Experience

Today my work centers on detection engineering — building scalable detection programs, writing detection content in Python, deploying detection content across cloud environments, and integrating threat intelligence to keep defenses current. I design automated response workflows that turn detection signals into action without waiting for human intervention, and I bring the same precision I developed doing hands-on infrastructure work to every layer of the stack.

My background spans both sides of the offense-defense equation. I have run red team campaigns, built custom tooling for exploit development, and driven purple team exercises that pressure-test real infrastructure. Earlier in my career I delivered penetration tests and designed malware analysis pipelines — experience that now anchors how I think about resilient detection strategy. Understanding how attackers move is what makes detection engineering more than rule writing.

Community

I enjoy giving back through precise technical writing and security research. You can find more of my writing at https://blog.defcesco.io/. You can explore my published work and conference talks on the publications page.
